Naan Bread

You naan’t believe how delicious this bread is! Naan is a soft, pillowy flatbread that originated in Southern and Central Asia, with deep roots in Indian cuisine. It’s a yeast-leavened bread traditionally cooked in a tandoor—a blazing-hot clay oven—where small pieces of dough are stretched by hand and slapped onto the oven’s walls to cook. The result? A beautifully charred, slightly chewy bread that’s perfect for scooping up curries, dips, or even enjoying on its own.

Prep time

60 min.

Cook time

15 min.

serving

8 servings

Tags : Bread Naan food

INSTRUCTIONS

Step 1

Prepare the dough, mixing the dry ingredients first then adding the wet ingredients. Knead for 15 minutes then let it rest for 1-2 hours

Step 2

Once risen, shape the naan by rolling it out into 6-8 equal balls. Roll each one out into an oval shape 1/4 inch thick

Step 3

Mix some butter with 2 cloves of garlic to be used to brush on the Naan

Step 4

Cook the Naan on a hot skillet for 1-2 minutes until bubbles start to form, then flip and cook the other side til golden brown

Step 5

Brush the Naan with the garlic butter and serve with a side of your choice

ingredients

2 cups all purpose flour

1/2 teaspoon salt

1 teaspoon sugar and active dry yeast

1/4 cup plain yoghurt

1/2 cup warm water

garlic and butter

tools

Stainless steel mixing bowl

Measuring Spoons

Measuring Cups
